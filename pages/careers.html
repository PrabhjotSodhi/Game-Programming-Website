STAGES

STAGE I

When Working for a indie company that you or someone else owns, is where most people start there game develop- ment career. Here you have less strain as you ease into the work. Since Indie companies don't have big budgets and are small teams of 1 - 5, they hire you for low amounts and expect you to produce most of the gameplay progra- mming features due to lack of enough programmers. These features are normally basic as Indie companies don't ask you to make Triple AAA features

STAGE II

This is a tier that is classified as not being a indie team but also not a triple AAA company as well making it the middle ground. Here you have deadlines to meet. These companies have big enough pockets to invest into features and teams of 20 - 100, they hire you for decent amounts and expect you to output results that live up to their standards. This time your not alone. you have other programmers creating different features and are more synced with each other. You need experience in other companies to go in this league

STAGE III

If you are hoping on getting that sweet sweet cash, you need to work in this level. Here deadlines mean life and death for you and you better produce results. These companies have millions to invest into their game and will get the most out of it. They hire you for large amounts and expect you to go past their expectations The environment is vastly different, you will have lead programmers telling you want to do and you will obey without second thought. you are expected to work hours on end fine tuning the current system with tens to hundreds of other programmers.
